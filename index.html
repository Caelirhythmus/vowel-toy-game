Rules

  • Words are CVCV structure. Consonants (C) are from: b, p, m, d, t, n, h, g, k.
  • Vowels (V) are from: i, e, ɛ, æ, a (in increasing opening degree: i < e < ɛ < æ < a).
  • Consonants in the same position are identical in both words.
  • Change is possible only if for each vowel position, the opening degree of A's vowel is greater than or equal to that of B's vowel (i.e., change from larger opening to smaller opening is allowed).
  • If any vowel position violates this rule, the whole word cannot change.
